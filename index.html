Thank you for visiting my portfolio. Before scrolling down and checking out some of my works, I will like to share a bit about myself. I have 5+ years of experience assisting in research, maintaining and troubleshooting computers, providing cataloging and related technical services, and assisting with technology usage for teaching and learning. I also take an active interest in community development and have convinced a former employer to set up library van projects for two rural communities as part of its corporate responsibility strategy, and which I successfully managed until 2014. Besides an MLIS, I hold a Master of Science degree in Forestry from the University of British Columbia where I worked with Dr. Terry Sunderland at the Sunderland Lab on a dissertation that explored solutions for disseminating conservation information in high forest zone natural resource-dependent communities. Research-wise, I am interested in exploring trends and issues in data management for scientific publication. I am also curious about areas like knowledge organization and computer-mediated social discourse. I use bibliometrics, machine learning, and text mining and analytic techniques to explore communication patterns and social interactions occurring in various platforms and genres such as social media, online blogs, tweets, and virtual reference chat activities. Generally, I see myself as an all-round information professional capable of serving effectively in any information role I find myself in. However, in previous experiences in academia and industry, my strengths have been successfully exploited in information-focused roles requiring supporting teaching and learning, technology usage, technical and scientific information management, or strategic decision making.
